PLAY MY GAME

I am not much of a gamer. But I like games. Games are part of everything we do. Just another problem to be solved. Except with a game you get to choose what flavour of problem and that is based on your own bias for what problems you enjoy.

PLAY FUGUSHIMA Not for mobile

PLAY REMEMBER For mobile (unskinned)

Well as a creative who has been trained to think in terms of Marketing and Advertising principles. I can't help be drawn to the seductive possibilities game design offers. Platform games are out of my reach at this point. So instead I gravitate towards browser style games and try to keep an ear to the ground when new advances are realised. I believe it is only a matter of time before they merge.

One of these advances is ASM a pre-compiled subset of Javascript that runs like native C code in the browser. Very cool, but still a bit advanced for me. The open sourcing of the Unreal Engine and projects like Emsripten start to bring it all a lot closer.

In the mean time I have been busying myself with less complex frameworks and 2D experiences using WebGL. There are many Javascript game frameworks out there but I initially choose to focus on Phaser, as it closely resembles Adobe Flash, which I am used to working with and has a good selection of tools to get the job done. They haven't modularised it yet but I think that will be coming soon.

Unity is also another interesting offering as they have recently brought webGL rendering to there engine allowing for non plug-in based games to be played in the browser. Anyway you can checkout the first game I made with Phaser above and another freelance project that is unskinned but still playable. I am currently working on another larger game in my spare time, but because of it's size I have been side tracked by learning how to better manage my code through modularisation.
